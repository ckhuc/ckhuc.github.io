Ethics

Ethics is important because technology affects people's privacy, safety, and daily lives. Developers and technology professionals should think carefully about how their work impacts users and society.

Privacy and Data Protection

One major ethical responsibility in IT is protecting user data. Developers should only collect data that is necessary, keep that data secure, and be honest with users about how their information is being used.

Security

Technology professionals should design systems that are safe and reliable. Weak security can expose users to identity theft, financial harm, or loss of personal information.

Bias and Fairness

Data scientists and software developers should also consider bias. If a system is trained on unfair or incomplete data, it may produce unfair results. Ethical technology should be tested carefully to reduce harm.

Professional Responsibility

The ACM Code of Ethics explains that computing professionals should contribute to society, avoid harm, be honest, respect privacy, and honor confidentiality. These ideas are important because technology workers often make decisions that affect many people.
