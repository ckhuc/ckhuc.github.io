Ethics

Ethics is important as technology influences people’s privacy, safety, and daily lives. Therefore, it is critical for developers and other professionals who use technology to consider the impact of their actions on both their customers/users, and society.

Privacy and Data Protection

Protecting user data is one major ethical obligation in the area of Information Technology. In general, developers should only collect data when it is required to fulfill a specific function; protect that collected data by keeping it secure; and clearly communicate with customers/clients regarding how their data will be utilized.

Security

Professionals should create products/systems which are designed to ensure they are safe and reliable. As an example, weak security in applications can result in user exposure to identity theft, financial loss and/or theft of personal information.

Bias and Fairness

Developers using machine learning algorithms also need to address issues related to "ias. A system utilizing a biased algorithm may provide outcomes which are not fair. Therefore, developers should test these systems thoroughly to minimize potential harm.

Professional Responsibility

ACM's Code of Ethics addresses computing professionals' obligations including contributing to society, avoiding harm, honesty, respect for privacy, and maintaining confidentiality. The above items are significant due to technology employees often having responsibilities which affect large numbers of people.
